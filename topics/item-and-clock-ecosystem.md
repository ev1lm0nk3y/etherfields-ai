# Topic: Item Ecosystem & The Clock Counter

This topic covers the communal **Item Ecosystem** and the management of the macro **Clock Counter**.

---

## 1. Item Ecosystem & communal Slots
Items in *Etherfields* represent team-owned mystical oddities scattered through the Dreamworld.

* **Communal Assets:** All active Items are entirely **communal** (Rulebook v2.0, pg 11). They do not belong to individual players.
* **Active Slots:** There is a maximum of **3 Active Item slots** on the board (4 slots once unlocked). Any Dreamer can choose to use any of these active items.
* **Using Items:** During the Dreamers Phase, any player can use an active item's printed action or discard it for its printed Intent values (pg 11).
* **Readying & Exhausting:** If an item's printed action is used and is not discarded, it is turned sideways (**Exhausted**). At the start of the **Dreamers Phase** of each subsequent turn, all exhausted items are automatically **Readied** (straightened out).
* **Storage Slot:** All unused gained items sit face-down in the shared **Storage Slot**. When an effect says "Restore an Item", draw a random item from Storage and place it in an Active slot.
* **Consumables:** If an item explicitly dictates to discard after use, return it to the global **Storage** or **Item Market** as specified, not to any player's deck.

---

## 2. The Clock Counter & Effort
The Clock track is a global timer that counts down your available hours during Dreams and Slumbers.

* **Setup:** At the start of a Dream or Slumber, the Hour Counter is set on the Clock track based on player count (pg 13):
  * **1 Player:** 5 Hours
  * **2 Players:** 4 Hours
  * **3 Players:** 3 Hours
  * **4 Players:** 2 Hours
  * **5 Players:** 1 Hour
* **Spending Hours (The "Effort" Basic Action):** Once per turn, any Dreamer can burn hours on the clock to gain movement:
  * **Action:** *"Lose X Hours to move X spaces"* (pg 9, pg 20).
* **Other Hour Modifications:**
  * **Secret Scripts:** Narrative script choices frequently cause the team to gain or lose Hours based on story outcomes.
  * **Advanced Cards:** Later campaign mechanics, Fate cards, and Lucid upgrades can directly add or subtract Hours on the Clock track.
  * **Resetting Hours:** Hours are reset to their default maximums during **Awakening** (pg 13) or the **Delta Phase** (pg 15).
